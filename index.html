Our Process

Designing into the Future

While forward thinking, our design approach is sound with optimized tools and advanced technology to ensure work efficiency as well as lower costs. Our workflow primarily uses a single software platform to handle project deliverables from standard plan sets to 3D visualization. In the early stage of the project and all the way through the process, you will be able to fully understand the design in multi-dimensions like in real life. With Cité Designs, the visualization of the project is an integral part of the process, not an optional costly add-on service.

An Innovative and Cutting-Edge Workflow

To optimize its services, Cité Designs has adopted the SketchUp & Layout for Architecture workflow. SketchUp & Layout for Architecture has been developed by the best experts in the field. This workflow belongs to the latest generation of design tools that allow to approach design in all its dimensions from the get-go. Instead of a series of 2-dimensional drawings, your project is a virtual 3D design. At the center is a 3D model from which will be generated all standard drafting views such as floor plans, elevations, and sections. Changes to design are integrated in real time, making the process more fluid and more interactive.

ADUs

Additional Dwelling Units

Most cities and counties in California make it easier for homeowners to consider adding units to their property. Additional square footage and expedited process are by law required for this type of project. Given the state of housing in California cities, this is a good solution to provide additional units on the market as well as helping homeowners invest in their properties, increase available floor area (e.g. to add a workspace), and long-term planning options for additional source of income. Depending on each situation, an ADU might be within an existing building or structure, an addition, or a detached structure in most cases in the rear side of the property. Overall many design options are available to adapt to a variety of conditions and owners' needs.

Attached ADUs

Conveniently, attached ADUs can be a conversion of non-habitable space (e.g. garages), or additional space allowing to increase property square footage and new unit opportunities.
